Scramble Eggs

Description

Scrambled eggs are eggs beaten and cooked in a pan until softly set, often with milk or cream, seasoned with salt and pepper. They're stirred as they cook to create creamy curds. They're a versatile dish enjoyed on their own or with added ingredients like cheese, vegetables, or meats.

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Milk
  • Butter

Steps

  1. Crack the desired number of eggs into a bowl. The usual serving size is about 2-3 eggs per person.
  2. Use a fork or whisk to beat the eggs until the yolks and whites are well mixed and the mixture appears slightly frothy.
  3. Add a splash of milk to the beaten eggs. The milk helps make the eggs fluffier and creamier. Use roughly 1-2 tablespoons of milk per 2-3 eggs.
  4. Sprinkle a pinch of salt and a dash of pepper into the egg mixture. Adjust the amount according to your taste preferences.
  5. Whisk or stir the egg mixture again to ensure that the milk, salt, and pepper are evenly distributed.
  6. Place a non-stick skillet or frying pan over medium-low heat. Add a small amount of butter or oil and let it melt and coat the bottom of the pan.
  7. Pour the egg mixture into the heated pan.
  8. As the eggs start to cook, use a spatula to gently stir and scrape the eggs from the edges of the pan towards the center. This will create small curds.
  9. Adjust the heat as needed to prevent the eggs from cooking too quickly. The goal is to have gentle, slow cooking for creamy scrambled eggs.
  10. Continue stirring and cooking the eggs until they are softly set but still slightly creamy. If you prefer drier eggs, cook them a bit longer.
  11. Once the eggs are cooked to your liking, immediately remove the pan from the heat. The residual heat will continue to cook the eggs slightly.
  12. Plate the scrambled eggs and enjoy them while they're still warm.
